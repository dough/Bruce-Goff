## Facets of my relationship with this mentor and friend

In 1958 I left Rice after 2 years to join him in Bartlesville, and then Kansas City. The creative world was new to me, and much more interesting at that time than school, and I planned to remain in mathematics but learn more about creating.

    As I began to contribute more and more to the work of actually getting buildings constructed (which meant in particular trying to help BG meet budgets) and the work involved travelling to jobsites without him, the issue of we will have to check with the great man began to arise. A relatively easy solution was to have my name on the construction drawings. There would clearly never be any doubt whether the creativity was his, and the original conception of how things were to be was totally his, I had no claim in any way to be an architect, but he had learned that meant I would never try to express myself creatively in the work, and did have a certain amount of common sense that sometimes helped get them actually built!
